## The Problem (Start Here — use Zenline language)

Category teams see demand **too late**. By the time POS and inventory tell the story, competitors already own the shelf.

Zenline's platform already combines **internal data** (sales, margin, PIM, inventory) with **external signals** (search, marketplaces, competitor assortment, social). Our hackathon build demos the **external Scout layer** for Swiss outdoor retail — the part that answers: *what's emerging before our sales data shows it?*

External signals show up everywhere:

- Search velocity rising for `gravel laufschuhe` abroad  
- Competitor assortment gaps on Transa vs global launches  
- Regulation shifts (PFAS) changing what customers expect  
- Social and trade press noise that must be filtered before buying  

**The hard part is not finding data. The hard part is deciding what to do next — without cannibalizing your existing range.**

**Our job today:** show the jury Scout + Overlap Guard + Range Architect for a Swiss outdoor retailer:

1. What is emerging?  
2. Where did it start?  
3. Can we trust the evidence?  
4. Will it work in Switzerland?  
5. What should we do next — test, buy, contact a supplier, or just monitor?

## How It Works (Simple Version)

Use this 5-step story in conversations and on stage:

```
1. COLLECT     →  Gather signals from the web, search trends, and Swiss competitor shelves
2. GROUP       →  Merge overlapping mentions into one clear opportunity theme
3. VERIFY      →  Keep only themes backed by multiple independent sources
4. LOCALIZE    →  Score whether the trend fits Switzerland / DACH
5. RECOMMEND   →  Output a ranked list with a clear retail action
```

### The insight in plain language

**We do not rank individual posts or articles.**

We ask: *“Do several independent sources, in several markets, point to the same opportunity — and is there still room for us in Switzerland?”*

If the answer is no → we label it low confidence and say **monitor**, not **buy**.

That is how we turn **noise into a decision**.

## Why Switzerland / DACH Matters in the Story

Every strong opportunity in our demo should answer:

> **“Why this, why now, and why here?”**

Swiss outdoor retail is a great example because trends must pass extra filters:

- **Climate** — alpine, wet, variable conditions  
- **Price** — premium market with specific price bands  
- **Assortment gaps** — what Transa, Ochsner Sport, Decathlon CH already carry  
- **Regulation & culture** — e.g. PFAS-free gear, sustainability, EU-aligned rules  

**Our differentiator:** we do not just spot global hype. We explain **transferability** — whether a trend can realistically work in CH/DACH.

## Demo Stories We Can Tell (Anchor Examples)

Use 2–3 of these in the pitch. Each one follows the same narrative shape.

### Story template

1. **Signal** — what we noticed emerging  
2. **Evidence** — where we saw it (with sources)  
3. **Gap** — what Swiss retailers are missing  
4. **Action** — what the buyer should do next  
5. **Risk** — what we still do not know  

---

### Example 1: PFAS-free waterproof gear

- **Signal:** PFAS-free membranes are gaining traction as regulations and consumer expectations shift.  
- **Evidence:** Trade coverage + search momentum + product launches in US/EU markets.  
- **Gap:** Limited dedicated PFAS-free positioning in local CH assortment.  
- **Action:** Pilot a focused PFAS-free shell category in 1–2 stores; contact 2–3 brands already leading in EU.  
- **Risk:** Need to validate price acceptance and technical performance claims in alpine use.

**Why this story works:** strong “why now” (regulation + sustainability) and clear Swiss relevance.

---

### Example 2: Ultralight fast-hiking kits

- **Signal:** “Fast hiking” / ultralight day systems growing in US and Japan before mainstream DACH adoption.  
- **Evidence:** Publications, search trends, early marketplace traction abroad.  
- **Gap:** Niche still under-merchandised vs traditional hiking assortment in CH.  
- **Action:** Test-buy a curated ultralight capsule; monitor sell-through for 8 weeks.  
- **Risk:** Niche size in Switzerland — may stay enthusiast-only.

**Why this story works:** classic “early in US/JP, late in CH” transfer story.

---

### Example 3: Gravel / trail hybrid footwear

- **Signal:** Hybrid shoes bridging trail running and lifestyle use are growing in multiple markets.  
- **Evidence:** Social usage, marketplace listings, brand launches.  
- **Gap:** Assortment exists but often not positioned as a distinct trend lane.  
- **Action:** Create a dedicated in-store “gravel trail” bay with 6–8 SKUs; A/B test messaging.  
- **Risk:** Trend may overlap with existing running SKUs without incremental sales.

---

### Example 4: Packable synthetic insulation

- **Signal:** Packable synthetic insulation gaining share where wet conditions matter.  
- **Evidence:** Product launches, reviews, search interest in wet-climate markets.  
- **Gap:** Swiss alpine customers need wet-performance stories, not just “light down.”  
- **Action:** Merchandise as “wet alpine layer” with staff training; monitor conversion vs down.  
- **Risk:** Margin and brand mix constraints with existing suppliers.

## What Makes Us Credible (Not Just AI Hype)

Say these clearly — they matter to business and jury audiences:

1. **Every recommendation links to real sources** — URLs the jury can click  
2. **Weak signals stay weak** — we do not over-recommend  
3. **Multiple source types beat one viral clip** — web + search + competitor check  
4. **The scoring is transparent** — we explain confidence and limitations  
5. **The system reruns** — change category/keywords → new ranked output  

**Soundbite:**

> *“We don’t ask AI what’s trendy. We make AI gather evidence, then our system decides what’s strong enough to act on in Switzerland.”*

## Language Cheat Sheet

**Use this wording** → **Avoid this wording**

| Prefer | Avoid |
|---|---|
| “Evidence-backed recommendation” | “AI said this is trending” |
| “Confidence level” | “Accuracy score” (sounds fake-precise) |
| “Test-buy / pilot / monitor” | “Invest immediately” |
| “Transferability to CH/DACH” | “Global trend” (too vague) |
| “Assortment gap” | “Nobody sells this” (too absolute) |
| “Emerging opportunity” | “The next big thing” (hype) |
| “Reusable system” | “Platform” (overused, vague) |

## Handling Tough Questions

### “Isn’t this just ChatGPT research?”

> No. ChatGPT can write a trend report. We built a **repeatable pipeline** that collects structured signals, requires cross-source corroboration, scores Swiss fit, and outputs ranked actions with evidence the jury can inspect.

### “How do you know it’s not noise?”

> Single-source spikes get filtered out. We only promote clusters with multiple independent URLs and source types. Weak clusters stay in the data as “monitor,” not “buy.”

### “Why should a Swiss retailer care about US/Japan trends?”

> Because outdoor trends often appear there first. Our value is spotting them **early** and assessing whether they **transfer** before competitors move.

### “What if the demo fails live?”

> The dashboard reads our latest saved results. Even offline, the jury can inspect ranked opportunities and click evidence links.

### “Can this work outside outdoor retail?”

> Yes. The inputs are configurable — market, category, keywords, competitors. Same pipeline, different scenario.
